UCL/YUNG YiDiSH Spring School  in Contemporary Spoken Yiddish and Hasidic Culture
Tel Aviv, 10-16 May 2020

a week-long immersion course for intermediate and advanced learners in spoken Yiddish learning about contemporary Hasidic culture

LEARN MORE INVITATION BY MENDY CAHAN

AT A GLANCE

Morning

We will spend mornings together in the library. You can choose to either volunteer with library work or have some study time exploring the books in the collection. In any case, we will continuously speak Yiddish. Coffee/tea and light food will be available.

Afternoon

Mendy Cahan will teach the group for three hours every afternoon. The material will concern historical and current varieties of Yiddish another focus will be contemporary Hasidic Yiddish culture. If you are a university student and would like to attend our School for credit, UCL can certify this. 

Evening

There will be public lectures, concerts, cabarets and other performances at YUNG YiDiSH taking place on some of the evenings during the School. Your attendance is complimentary; any friend travelling with you can attend at a reduced rate. 

LagBeOmer

We will travel to the Meron for an overnight trip to take part in the so-called Hasidic Burning Man, where hundreds of thousands of Haredi people gather at LagBeOmer to sing, dance and celebrate together.

Shabes

We will mark the incoming of the Shabes, and have no formal teaching on the day of the shabes. But we will gather for shalesides at YUNG YiDiSH and close our week together with Havdula and a party on motse shabes.

Swinging Tel Aviv

Tel Aviv has everything to offer from perfect weather, to great cafes and bars, souks and of course, the Mediterranean.

More details

Spring School in Contemporary Spoken Yiddish and Hasidic Culture 2020 is co-organised by the HASYID research project from University College London and the YUNG YiDiSH Library & Cultural Centre in Tel Aviv.  

The YUNG YiDiSH
Library & Cultural Centre

YUNG YiDiSH Tel Aviv is on the 5th floor of Tel Aviv's Central Bus Station. It houses over 50 thousand Yiddish books, manuscripts, LPs, and artefacts. It has a lively cultural life with over a hundred Yiddish-language events every year. 

read more about YUNG YiDiSH

MENDY CAHAN

Mendy was born and raised in a Yiddish-speaking family in Antverpen, and later moved to Israel to study literature and philosophy. He is an expert on contemporary Haredi Yiddish culture. He has taught Yiddish language and culture at various international summer courses.




Learning activities


The group will spend the mornings at YUNG YiDiSH Tel Aviv together, talking Yiddish at all times! You can either volunteer your time to help with uncatalogued new arrivals in the library or use this time to explore the collection in your individual study time. Cardamom coffee from Jaffa, tea, water and some light food will always be available.

In the afternoons, Mendy Cahan will teach three-hour formal classes on historical and contemporary varieties of Yiddish and contemporary Haredi Yiddish culture. 




Evening cultural activities

There will be several lectures, concerts, cabarets taking place at YUNG YiDiSH during the Spring School. Your attendance is free of charge. If you are travelling together with a friend who is not attending the school, we offer reduced ticket prices.

TEL AVIV, ISRAEL

Taking part in the YUNG YiDiSH Tel Aviv Spring School gives you an opportunity to spend time in Tel Aviv. With its museums and famous sites, the many bars and cafes, guaranteed good weather and the Mediterranean sea front, you will definitely enjoy your stay.

LagBeOmer at the Meron

Hundreds of thousands of Haredi Jews come together every year at LagBeOmer to the Meron. We will drive up in the afternoon, in time for the lighting of the fire by the Boyan Rebbe. We will dance, sing and immerse in this truly unique Hasidic experience. We will arrive back in Tel Aviv in the morning and classes resume late in the afternoon.

Shabes





We will mark the arrival of the Shabes together and we will have no formal classes on the Shabes. But we will meet in the afternoon to eat, drink and sing together by a tish. We will mark the end of the Shabes and our week together by making Havdule. 





Party time

To mark the end of the School and our week together, we will meet on Shabes, we will make Havdule and throw a party together on Motse Shabes

Workshop fees

Coffee/ tea and simple food will always be available during the day free of charge. We will spend shabes together and finish the week off with a party! If you are a student and attend the course for credit, the price also includes course assessment.   • BE A TZADIK - if you can afford to pay the Supporter rate, at 2,400 shekels, this will  contribute to the upkeep of YUNG YiDiSH. YUNG YiDiSH maintains a lively cultural life in Yiddish in adverse financial conditions. It is the only organisation in Israel that tasks itself with the collection and preservation of Yiddish books from the public, with only a small proportion of its budget being covered by government sources.
